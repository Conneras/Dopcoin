System model

1) Onboarding and credentials

  • KYC, sanctions/PEP, and source-of-funds checks at entry.
  • Issue a revocable, non-transferable “Verified” credential.
  • Credential proves eligibility without exposing identity in everyday use.
  • Tiered access based on risk score and limits.

2) Private ledger with controlled settlement

  • Most transfers happen on an internal encrypted ledger.
  • Public-chain settlement runs in batches to reduce linkage.
  • Policy engine decides when settlement happens and for whom.
  • Clear receipts and dispute flows without excess data collection.

3) Selective disclosure

  • Users can prove eligibility, limits, or ownership without revealing full history.
  • Audit mode produces scoped, time-bound disclosures for specific cases.
  • Disclosure requires approvals and leaves an immutable internal audit trail.

Controls that stop abuse

Real-time defenses

  • Velocity limits, spending caps, and tier-based constraints.
  • Fraud scoring and anomaly detection.
  • Allowlist cohorts for early rollout.
  • Instant freeze hooks for confirmed abuse.

Governance

  • Human review for edge cases and escalations.
  • Quorum approval for audit mode or sensitive actions.
  • Strict access controls and logging.
  • Documented incident response.

Security and privacy defaults

Key management

  • Passkeys by default. Optional 2FA.
  • User keys stay client-side. Hardware-backed where possible.
  • Per-action keys and rotation.
  • Encrypted backups with user-controlled recovery.

Data minimization

  • Short retention windows for metadata.
  • Collect only what you need for the product and compliance scope.
  • No third-party analytics scripts.
  • First-party metrics only, opt-in.

FAQ

Is this “anonymous”?

No. Users are verified at entry. The goal is to reduce public exposure and tracking, not to hide illicit activity.

What does the token do?

If you use a token, make it a utility for fees or credits. Don’t market it as an identity-hiding tool.

Can you still support crypto rails?

Yes, where legal, and with controls. The default experience can stay off public ledgers via internal balances.

What about regulators?

You build audit mode and selective disclosure into the core so you can comply without mass surveillance.
